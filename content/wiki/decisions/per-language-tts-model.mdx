## The choice

`MODEL_BY_LANGUAGE` maps en/fr/es to `eleven_flash_v2_5`. Everything else (hi, hinglish, mr, pa, te, ta, bn) falls back to `eleven_multilingual_v2`.

## The numbers

| Model | Cost (per char, normalised) |
|---|---|
| `eleven_flash_v2_5` | 0.5× |
| `eleven_multilingual_v2` | 1× |

Flash is half the cost. If ~40% of newly authored content is EN/FR/ES, that's a ~20% reduction on the audio line item with no quality loss for those languages.

## Alternatives considered

- **All Flash** — half the cost across the board. But Flash mispronounces Devanagari / Tamil / Bengali conjuncts noticeably more often. For learners trying to absorb concepts, mispronunciation is worse than spending the extra credits.
- **All multilingual_v2** — known quantity. What we had before. Status quo cost.
- **Per-language manual tuning of voice settings** — `stability`, `similarity_boost`. Doesn't change cost; deferred until we have signal that the voice settings need work.

## Why we picked the split

Indic pronunciation is the highest-stakes quality factor for our learners. We'd rather pay 1× there. EN/FR/ES are Latin-script, the model handles them cleanly at 0.5×.

## What would have to change to reverse

One line in `lib/audio/voices.ts`:

```ts
const MODEL_BY_LANGUAGE: Record<string, string> = {};  // all fall back to TTS_MODEL
```

Or to push Flash everywhere, add every language code with `"eleven_flash_v2_5"` as the value.

## Cache implication

Cache key is `sha256(voiceId|model|text)`. Switching models invalidates the cache for the affected languages — first regeneration of an existing lesson pays the credit again. Acceptable one-time hit.
