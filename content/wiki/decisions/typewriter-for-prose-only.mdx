## The choice

The `<Typewriter>` component (`app/(app)/learn/[courseSlug]/[lessonSlug]/Typewriter.tsx`) reveals text one whitespace-delimited token at a time at ~120 ms/word. It's wrapped around exactly three places:

1. `tutor_message.content.text` (the main bubble).
2. AI conversation starter — the FIRST assistant bubble (`i === 0 && role === "assistant"`).
3. `checkpoint.content.summary`.

Everything else (MCQ option labels, free-text inputs, drag chips, fill-in-the-blank templates) renders instantly.

## Why word-by-word, not character-by-character

- **Conjunct scripts.** Devanagari, Tamil, and Bengali use conjuncts that look weird when revealed character-by-character. Whitespace-token reveal sidesteps the issue.
- **Pace feels right.** ~120 ms/word ≈ a calm reading speed.

## Why prose only

Animating MCQ option labels means the learner has to wait to read what they're picking from. They came to answer a question; making them wait is hostile.

The starter bubble of an AI conversation IS prose-y — it's Maya kicking off the chat. But the back-and-forth that follows (real model responses to user input) appears instantly because the learner is now in active dialogue.

## Tap to skip

Click anywhere on the typing text (or press Space / Enter while focused) immediately reveals the full message. Power users escape the animation in one tap.

## What we DIDN'T do

- **Sync to audio narration.** Beautiful when it works but needs word-level timestamps from ElevenLabs, which we don't have.
- **Apply to reflection prompts.** They're heading-style (24 px Fraunces), and animating a heading feels gimmicky.
- **Apply to all text including UI chrome.** Would feel chaotic.

## What would have to change to reverse

Remove the `<Typewriter>` wrappers in `LessonPlayer.tsx`. The component file can stay around for any future use.
