## The choice

`tierCanAccess()` models two independent tracks:

```
AI-tool ladder:   free ⊂ basic ⊂ advanced
School track:     free ⊂ student          (parallel, not nested)
```

A user on `advanced` does NOT automatically unlock `student` content. A user on `student` does NOT automatically unlock `basic` content.

## Alternatives considered

- **Nest `student` above `advanced`** — paying for advanced auto-grants school content. Simpler gate; one rank-ordered ladder.
- **Nest `advanced` above `student`** — the opposite.
- **Bundle them into an `all-access` plan** — one product, two audiences.

## Why we picked parallel

Adults buying `advanced` for ChatGPT / Cursor / Synthesia training don't need Class 10 Math. Bundling the school content into their plan would feel like padding. Conversely, a parent buying `student` for their kid doesn't want to pay for our AI-tool ladder.

The audiences barely overlap. Pricing them separately matches how the customer thinks about them, and lets us tune each track's price without affecting the other.

## What would have to change to reverse

- `tierCanAccess()` in `lib/types.ts` — change the per-tier rules.
- The `/profile` "current plan" rendering — would no longer need to list multiple plans.
- The `subscriptions` table — could in theory add a unique constraint on `user_id` again (we removed it for multi-plan).
