The learner picks a tutor persona from `/profile`. The persona drives:

- The avatar / glyph rendered in the lesson player and home dashboard.
- A small flavour bias in AI conversation turns (passed as part of the system prompt).
- Future: voice timbre (today all personas share the same ElevenLabs voice per language).

`PERSONAS` is defined in `lib/types.ts`.

| ID | Name | Tagline | Glyph |
|---|---|---|---|
| `nova` | Nova | Warm & patient | ✦ |
| `arjun` | Arjun | Direct & demanding | ◆ |
| `riya` | Riya | Chill & Hinglish | ❁ |
| `sensei` | Sensei | Old-school discipline | ☯ |

`nova` is the default. Stored on `profiles.preferred_tutor_persona`.

## Where the persona shows up

- **Lesson player** — `<NovaAvatar persona={…} />` next to each tutor bubble.
- **Home dashboard** — persona's avatar in the header.
- **AI conversation route** (`/api/ai/conversation`) — persona name + tagline are appended to the Claude system prompt as `"You are <persona.name>, a <persona.tagline> AI tutor."` to bias tone.
- **Profile page** — picker.

## "Maya" is the brand name, not a persona

The marketing surface and the math-quiz feedback call the tutor **Maya**. Maya is the public-facing name of the whole tutoring layer; Nova / Arjun / Riya / Sensei are sub-personalities a learner can pick. See [Maya](/wiki/glossary/maya).

## Avatar storage

Personas have an optional `avatar_url` pointing at the public `tutor` Supabase Storage bucket. Today only Nova has one (`tutor/nova.png` with a signed URL). The others fall back to a glyph block.
