The Student plan tier (`plan_tier='student'`) splits into three audience cohorts, each with its own marketing page and two price points (one subject / all subjects). All three sit on the same `student` plan in the `plans` table — the cohort difference is content gating via `school_class` + `institute`, not a separate billing tier today.

## Routes + pricing

| Cohort | Route | Short key | One subject | All subjects |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| School Student · Class 6–10 | `/student-plan-stt` | `stt` (school-to-tenth) | ₹59 / mo | ₹199 / mo |
| School Student · Class 11–12 | `/student-plan-eat` | `eat` (eleventh-and-twelfth) | ₹199 / mo | ₹499 / mo |
| College Student · Bachelors | `/student-plan-bach` | `bach` (bachelors) | ₹199 / mo | ₹499 / mo |

The pages are public (no auth gate), structured for Cashfree integration later. CTAs go to a `startCheckout()` server action that today redirects to `/checkout?plan=<id>` and tomorrow will create a Cashfree subscription session.

## Plan IDs (stable, used by Cashfree later)

```
school-6-10-one
school-6-10-all
school-11-12-one
school-11-12-all
college-bachelors-one
college-bachelors-all
```

Each cohort's `plans` list has one `recommended: true` (the all-subjects option) — rendered with the "Most chosen" badge and indigo accent.

## How a learner ends up here

1. From the landing page's pricing block (Setu Pro card → `/onboarding`, then plan-detail).
2. From inside `/student` when they're prompted to upgrade (planned).
3. Directly from a marketing URL we share externally.

## Cohort gating in the dashboard

The `/student` dashboard reads `profiles.school_class` + `profiles.institute` and shows only matching curriculum bundles. Pricing pages don't gate by anything — they're sales surfaces.
