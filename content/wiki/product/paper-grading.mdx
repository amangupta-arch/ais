`/math-quiz-test` is the proof-of-concept for paper grading. The flow:

1. Public page — no auth.
2. Ten algebra questions presented one at a time.
3. Learner solves on paper, taps **Camera** (mobile) or **Upload** (desktop).
4. Image is compressed client-side to ~1280 px / 0.85 JPEG (`compressImage()` via canvas).
5. POST to `/api/ai/math-quiz` with the equation, expected answer, language, and base64 image.
6. Route calls Claude `claude-sonnet-4-6` with an image content block + tool_use forcing `submit_grade`.
7. Response renders as a green or coral feedback card with detected answer, sentence-long feedback, and a list of mistakes (in the learner's chosen language).
8. End-of-quiz summary: total time, accuracy, per-question time, fastest / slowest question.

## Why tool_use, not JSON-in-text

An earlier version asked Claude to return raw JSON. It occasionally returned prose-wrapped JSON or got truncated mid-object on longer scripts (Devanagari, Tamil), and the regex strip + `JSON.parse` blew up. Switching to tool_use makes the schema non-negotiable — Claude either fills the tool's input or doesn't respond.

See [tool_use vs JSON parse](/wiki/decisions/tool-use-vs-json-parse).

## Why compress the image client-side

Vercel's serverless functions have a 4.5 MB body limit. Phone-camera JPEGs are 3–6 MB; base64 encoding adds 33% overhead. A naive upload returned `"Request Entity Too Large"` as plain text, the client `JSON.parse`d it, and threw `Unexpected token 'R'`. Canvas-based downscale to 1280 px brings shots to 200–400 KB.

See [client-side image compression](/wiki/decisions/client-side-image-compression).

## What's deliberately NOT done

- No persistence — images are forwarded to Anthropic in-memory and dropped.
- No subscription gating — the page is public to demo the feature.
- No tutoring loop — it's a one-shot grader, not a multi-turn back-and-forth.

The next-step product surface is integrating this into the real lesson player as a turn type — students could see a "Solve on paper" turn inside the Class 10 Math course.
